Quick Overview

  1. Check Responsiveness: Tap the person and shout, “Are you okay?” Look for breathing or movement.
  2. Call Emergency Services: Call 911 or send someone to get help and locate an AED if available.
  3. Begin Chest Compressions: Place the heel of your hand on the lower half of the sternum and compress the chest 100–120 times per minute to a depth of 2–2.4 inches (5–6 cm).
  4. Open the Airway: Use the head-tilt chin-lift unless spinal injury is suspected. If trauma is suspected, use the jaw-thrust maneuver.
  5. Provide Rescue Breaths: Give 2 breaths after every 30 compressions if trained, watching for chest rise.
  6. Continue CPR: Continue compressions and breaths, use an AED if available, and keep going until help arrives or the person shows signs of life.

Gather Patient Information

If other people are present, ask for important information about the patient while CPR is being performed. This information can help emergency responders provide faster and more effective care when they arrive.

  • What happened? Ask witnesses what they saw before the person collapsed.
  • When did the person collapse? Knowing the time helps responders estimate how long the patient has been unresponsive.
  • Medical conditions: Ask if the patient has any known medical problems such as heart disease, diabetes, or seizures.
  • Medications: Find out if the patient takes any regular medications.
  • Allergies: Ask about any known allergies.
  • Recent symptoms: Ask if the patient complained of chest pain, shortness of breath, dizziness, or other symptoms before collapsing.

Share this information with emergency medical personnel when they arrive.
